What is a dataset?

A dataset is a collection of related data points typically organized in tables, arrays or specific formats for analysis. Datasets can contain various types of data, such as numerical values, categorical information, text, images, or time series data. They are used in various fields, including statistics, machine learning, and data science, to extract insights, identify patterns, and make informed decisions based on the information contained within them.

What is a distribution?

A distribution in statistics is a function that shows the possible values for a variable and how often they occur, so it describes how data points are spread or arranged. Using that it's possible to analyze the frequency of different outcomes, identify patterns, and make predictions based on the data.

Caesar cipher

A Caesar cipher is a simple encryption technique where each letter in the plaintext is shifted a fixed number of places down or up the alphabet. Defined a shift value, for example 3, each letter is replaced by the letter that is three positions later in the alphabet. For example, with a shift of 3, 'A' becomes 'D', 'B' becomes 'E', and so on. The cipher wraps around at the end of the alphabet, so 'X' becomes 'A', 'Y' becomes 'B', and 'Z' becomes 'C'. Non-letter characters, such as spaces and punctuation, are typically left unchanged.

Break caesar cipher using the Chi-squared test

The Chi-squared test can be used to break a Caesar cipher by analyzing the frequency distribution of letters in the ciphertext and comparing it to the expected frequency distribution of letters in the target language (e.g., English). By shifting the ciphertext through all possible values (1-25) and calculating the Chi-squared statistic for each shift, it's possible to identify the shift that results in the lowest Chi-squared value, indicating the best match to the expected letter frequencies. This shift is likely the key used in the Caesar cipher, allowing for decryption of the ciphertext back into plaintext. To calculating the Chi-squared statistic, the formula is: \[ \chi^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{26} \frac{(O_i - E_i)^2}{E_i} \] where \(O_i\) is the observed frequency of letter \(i\) of the alphabet in the ciphertext, and \(E_i\) is the expected frequency of letter \(i\) in English.
