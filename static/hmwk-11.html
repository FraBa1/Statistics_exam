Euler-Maruyama method

The Euler-Maruyama method is a numerical technique used to approximate solutions to stochastic differential equations (SDEs). It is an extension of the Euler method for ordinary differential equations (ODEs) and is particularly useful for simulating paths of stochastic processes, such as the Wiener process, also called Brownian motion. By discretizing the time interval and iteratively updating the solution using both deterministic and stochastic components, the Euler-Maruyama method provides a way to approximate the behavior of systems influenced by random noise.

Mathematically, many stochastic processes can be represented through a stochastic differential equation (SDE) of the form: \[ dX(t) = \mu(X(t), t)\,dt + \delta(X(t), t)\,dW(t) \]

Using the Euler-Maruyama method, the discrete version of the SDE becomes: \[ X_{n+1} = X_n + \mu(X_n, t_n)\,\Delta t + \delta(X_n, t_n)\,\Delta W_n \]

where:

  • \( X_n \) is the current value of the process at time \( t_n \)
  • \( \mu(X_n, t_n) \) is the drift coefficient, representing the deterministic part of the process
  • \( \delta(X_n, t_n) \) is the diffusion coefficient, representing the stochastic part of the process
  • \( \Delta t \) is the time step size
  • \( \Delta W_n = \sqrt{\Delta t}\,Z_n \), where \( Z_n \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1) \), so that \( \Delta W_n \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \Delta t) \)

Wiener process

A Wiener process, also known as Brownian motion, is a continuous-time stochastic process, \( W(t) \), that satisfies the following conditions:

  • \( W(0) = 0 \)
  • \( W(t) \) has independent increments from the other values
  • \( W(t) \) has stationary increments that depend only on the length of the time interval and not on the starting point
  • \( W(t) \) follows a normal distribution with mean 0 and variance \( t \), that is \( W(t) \sim \mathcal{N}(0, t) \)
  • \( W(t) \) has continuous paths with respect to time

The Wiener process can be obtained as a special case of the Euler-Maruyama scheme by choosing: \[ \mu(X_n, t_n) = 0, \quad \delta(X_n, t_n) = 1 \]

which leads to the discrete update rule used in simulations: \[ X_{n+1} = X_n + \Delta W_n \]
